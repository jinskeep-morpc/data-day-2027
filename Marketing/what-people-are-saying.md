# What People Say About Data Day

*Data Day is Central Ohio's annual conference for the public-sector data community. Here's what attendees tell us, from MORPC's post-event surveys for 2025 and 2026.*

## By the numbers

- **4.4 out of 5** — overall attendee satisfaction in 2026, and **4.3 in 2025** — consistently high, two years running.
- **~200 attendees** each year — more than **400** across the last two events.
- **75+ organizations** represented, from the City of Columbus and Ohio State University to suburbs, counties, and nonprofits across the region.
- **5.0 out of 5** — the rating earned by top breakout sessions, with many others scoring well above 4.5.

## In their words

> "This was my first Data Day and I got a lot more out of it than expected. I really enjoyed meeting professionals from a variety of backgrounds, and all of the sessions I attended had useful and inspiring information."
> — *Data Day 2025 attendee*

> "It's refreshing and insightful to be among people who value the insight that can come from sound data used in ethical ways. The spirit of mutual learning and inspiration of Data Day is unique."
> — *Data Day 2025 attendee*

> "It was the right length and the right cadence — I didn't feel fatigued at the end of the day and enjoyed even the last session. The whole day was very well run. Kudos!"
> — *Data Day 2026 attendee*

## Who's in the room

A cross-sector audience of roughly 200 data professionals and decision-makers gathers for the day — from local governments, nonprofits, universities, and civic-minded businesses. Recent attendees came from the City of Columbus, Ohio State University, Ohio EPA, JobsOhio, COTA, United Way of Central Ohio, Smart Columbus, and CMHA, alongside dozens of suburbs, counties, and nonprofits.
