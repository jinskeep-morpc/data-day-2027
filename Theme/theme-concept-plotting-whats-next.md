# Data Day 2027 — Theme Concept: "Plotting What's Next" (DRAFT)

> **Status:** **SELECTED — lead theme for Data Day 2027.** Anniversary-based and
> forward-looking, with a strong, ownable visual hook. Visual identity in progress (see
> [design brief](../Design/design-brief.md)). Voice: outward MORPC/RDR —
> mission-forward and inviting.

---

## Theme: **Plotting What's Next**

***Data Day 2027 · Five years forward for Central Ohio's data community***

**The idea in one line:** After five years of gathering the data, we can finally see the
trend line — and now we plot the course ahead. "Plotting" works two ways at once: we
*plot points* to reveal the pattern, and we *plot a route* to where the region's data
community goes next.

**Why it's strong.** The double meaning is native to this audience — every analyst in the
room plots data, and "plotting a course" is pure forward motion. It quietly nods to GIS
and mapping, a recurring Data Day strength, without narrowing the theme to one topic. And
the milestone is built in: five years of points are exactly what let you draw the line.

---

## The narrative

For five years, Data Day has brought Central Ohio's local governments, nonprofits,
educational institutions, and civic-minded businesses together around a shared belief —
that better use of data improves the region. Each year added another point to the plot.
Five years in, the pattern is clear and the community is real. *Plotting What's Next*
turns those five data points into a trajectory: we read where we've been, then chart the
path forward — together.

---

## How it threads through the program

- **Program.** Frame the keynotes as *the trend line* — where the field is heading; a
  forward-looking opener and a "what's next" lunch keynote. Individual sessions are the
  points on the plot; they need not obey the theme, and the Regional Data Agenda
  reflection prompt carries the mission thread.
- **Champions.** The people plotting the course forward — the community that carries the
  region to its next waypoint. Strong fit with the Champions model as the program's own
  forward-looking evolution.
- **Marketing / comms.** "Connect the dots," "plot the course," "five points, one
  trajectory." Then-and-now stats become literal data points across five years rising
  into a forward line — an energetic, on-theme way to show growth.
- **Sponsorship.** Reframes the ask as *investing in the trajectory* — backing where the
  next points land, not underwriting a single event.
- **Visual motif.** Plotted points connecting into an upward, forward path; a
  scatter resolving into a route; map waypoints; axes and an upward trend line ending in
  an arrow (the basis of the logo mark). Clear, modern, and ownable.

## Subhead options

1. **"Five years forward for Central Ohio's data community."** *(working default)* —
   states the milestone and the forward gaze plainly.
2. **"Five years in, mapping the road ahead."** — leans into the route/GIS reading.
3. **"From five years of data to what comes next."** — spells out the points-to-trajectory idea.

> Guardrail for all derived copy: keep the tone forward-looking. Lean on the *plot-a-course*
> sense, not just *plot-the-points* — the point of the concept is momentum, not a retrospective
> chart of the past five years.

---

## Design implications (note for the [design brief](../Design/design-brief.md))

The visual motif is **plot / chart / route** language: data points resolving into a
forward, upward path; trend lines; map waypoints. The hero logo mark is a **minimal
scatter-plot** — small rising points with a smooth curved trend line ending in an arrow,
paired with the "Data Day 2027" wordmark. See the [design brief](../Design/design-brief.md)
for the full logo prompt and art-direction decisions.
