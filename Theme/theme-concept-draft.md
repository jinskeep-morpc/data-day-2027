## Theme (locked): **Five Years Forward**

***Data Day 2027 · Shaping what's next for Central Ohio's data community***

**The idea in one line:** the fifth anniversary isn't a scrapbook — it's a launch pad. We've spent
five years convening the region's data community; now the question is where we take it next.

**The narrative.** Over five years, Data Day has brought Central Ohio's local governments,
nonprofits, educational institutions, and civic-minded businesses together around a shared belief
— that better use of data improves quality of life for all residents. Five years in, that
community is real, and *Five Years Forward* turns the milestone into momentum: honoring how far the
region has come, then pointing the whole room at what's ahead.

---

## How it threads through the program

- **Program.** Frame the keynotes as *where the field is heading* — a forward-looking opener and a
  "what's next" lunch keynote. Individual sessions need not obey the theme; the Regional Data
  Agenda reflection prompt already carries the mission thread.
- **Champions.** Near-perfect fit — the Champions model *is* the forward-looking evolution
  (replacing the old monthly Working Group). "The community that carries us forward."
- **Marketing / comms.** Momentum language — countdown, "5 years in motion," then-and-now stats
  (growth from the first Data Day to 2027). An energetic hook for the biggest-growth year.
- **Sponsorship.** Reframes the ask as *investing in the next five years*, not underwriting a
  single event.
- **Visual motif.** Motion — arrows, a forward-leaning "5," a path/road, momentum lines. A clear,
  modern brief for designers.

---

## Subhead: selected

**Subhead: "Shaping what's next for Central Ohio's data community."** Names the community, points
ahead, and pairs with "Forward" without doubling the metaphor — warm but not stuffy.

> Guardrail for all derived copy: keep the tone forward-looking. Avoid sliding into "celebrating
> five years of building community" — that reverts to the stale, backward-looking voice this
> concept is meant to avoid.
