# Data Day 2027 — Session Tagging & Skill Levels (DRAFT)

> **Audience:** mixed. The **skill scale** is attendee-facing (agenda + brochure). The **focus
> area, action pillar, and reflection prompt** are **internal CFP metadata** for MORPC staff
> curation — captured at submission, **not printed or used for scheduling/tracks**.
> **Status:** working draft of the tagging model that feeds the CFP and the program.

---

## How this connects to the Regional Data Agenda

Data Day 2027 ties the program to the **Regional Data Agenda** by asking every breakout speaker,
at submission, to locate their session within the Agenda's frameworks. This is **not a track
system** — there are no topical tracks, no per-area rooms, and no area labels in the printed
agenda. The tags exist so MORPC staff can understand each proposal and **curate a program that
spans the region's priorities**.

---

## What speakers provide at submission (internal — staff curation only)

1. **Focus area** — pick the one that best fits (the Agenda's four focus areas):
   - Transportation & Mobility
   - Housing & Community Development
   - Infrastructure & Economic Development
   - Energy & Sustainability
2. **Action pillar** — pick the one that best fits (the Agenda's four action pillars):
   - Promote Reliable & Trusted Data
   - Expand Education & Outreach
   - Bolster Project & Program Support
   - Increase Representation & Connection
3. **Reflection prompt** (short answer):
   > **"How does your session advance [selected Focus Area] as a regional data priority?"**

The focus area captures the **subject**; the pillar captures the **data practice**. Together they
give staff a quick, two-dimensional read on each submission and a way to check the program's
balance across the Agenda. None of these three appear in attendee-facing materials.

---

## What attendees see: the technical skill scale (0–3)

A single skill tag appears on each session in the agenda and brochure, so attendees can choose
sessions that match their comfort level.

| Level | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **0** | All audiences | No technical background needed; conceptual, strategic, or policy-oriented. |
| **1** | Beginner | Some data familiarity helpful; introduces a tool or method. |
| **2** | Intermediate | Assumes working knowledge; hands-on with specific tools/methods. |
| **3** | Advanced | For experienced practitioners; deep or specialized technical content. |

Speakers self-select a level at submission; staff may adjust for consistency across the program.

---

## Where each tag lives

| Tag | Captured at | Visible to attendees? | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Focus area | Submission | No | Staff curation / program balance |
| Action pillar | Submission | No | Staff curation / program balance |
| Reflection answer | Submission | No (may quietly inform session descriptions) | Curation; helps staff understand intent |
| Skill level (0–3) | Submission (speaker self-selects) | **Yes** — agenda + brochure | Helps attendees choose sessions |

---

## Which formats carry which tags

| Format | Focus area | Action pillar | Reflection | Skill level (0–3, public) | Set by |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Lecture** (open CFP) | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes | Speaker self-selects |
| **Workshop** (open CFP) | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes | Speaker self-selects |
| **Panel** (curated) | Yes | Yes | No | Yes | **Staff-assigned** |
| **Poster** (intern + student) | — | — | — | — | **No tags** |

- **Panels** join the program-balance picture: staff assign the (internal) focus area + pillar and
  a (public) skill level, but there's no speaker reflection since panels are curated.
- **Posters** carry **no tags** — they're browsable displays, not leveled time-grid sessions.

---

## Notes / open items

- **Reflection answer reuse:** internal by default, but well-written answers can seed the public
  session description — decide whether to tell speakers their answer may be lightly edited for
  public use.
- **Skill-level consistency:** because self-selection drifts, staff should normalize levels once
  the program is assembled.
