## Plotting What's Next
### *Five years forward for Central Ohio's data community*

Data Day returns for its **fifth year** on **Tuesday, February 16, 2027** — and this milestone is
a launch pad, not a look back. Across five years, Data Day has convened Central Ohio's public-sector
data community — **local governments, nonprofits, educational institutions, and civic-minded
businesses** — around a shared vision: **to make Central Ohio a leader in using data to improve
the quality of life for all residents.** *Plotting What's Next* turns that momentum toward what
comes next, and we want you in the room helping shape it.

There are many ways to take part — whether you want to present your work, lead a hands-on
session, share a research poster, or connect with peers across the region. Explore the formats
below and find the one that fits.

## Present at Data Day

### Lecture sessions
*Open call for proposals*

The backbone of the program. A lecture is a **45-minute breakout** (with time built in for
questions and discussion) in which you share a project, method, dataset, tool, or lesson learned
with a room of practitioners. Sessions span all skill levels — from introductory to advanced —
and we welcome proposals from across the four constituencies.

- **Format:** 45 minutes, including Q&A.
- **Rooms:** Terrace A, Terrace B, or the Grand Ballroom.
- **Who can propose:** anyone — individuals or teams.
- **What to tell us:** title, objectives, description, intended audience and skill level, and
  speaker(s). *(Full question list under "What we'll ask you to submit" below.)*

### Hands-on workshops
*Open call for proposals*

A **90-minute, technically focused working session** where participants follow along and build
something — a workshop is for skills, tools, and methods that are best learned by doing.
Workshops are held in a classroom-style room with tables and power; attendees are expected to
bring a laptop.

- **Format:** 90 minutes, hands-on; participants work along with the facilitator.
- **Room:** Grand Ballroom B (technical setup — tables, power).
- **Who can propose:** anyone comfortable leading an interactive, applied session.
- **What to tell us:** title, learning goals, the tools/skills covered, any prerequisites or
  setup attendees need, and the skill level.

### Panel discussions
*Curated by the program committee*

A **moderated, multi-speaker conversation** on a regional data topic, assembled by the
[program committee] to bring balanced perspectives to the stage. Panels are not part of the open
call — but if you have a topic or potential panelists to suggest, we'd love to hear from you.

- **Format:** 45 minutes, moderated, multiple speakers.
- **Room:** Grand Ballroom.
- **How to take part:** **[suggest a panel topic or panelist →]** *(link / form TBD).*

### Choosing a skill level

Every session carries a **technical skill level (0–3)** in the agenda so attendees can find
sessions that match their comfort. When you submit, tell us where your session fits:

| Level | Who it's for |
|---|---|
| **0 — All audiences** | No technical background needed; conceptual, strategic, or policy-oriented. |
| **1 — Beginner** | Some data familiarity helpful; introduces a tool or method. |
| **2 — Intermediate** | Assumes working knowledge; hands-on with specific tools/methods. |
| **3 — Advanced** | For experienced practitioners; deep or specialized technical content. |

We aim for a program that spans all four levels — entry points for newcomers *and* depth for
seasoned practitioners are equally welcome.

## Spotlight on emerging talent

### Intern Showcase — lightning talks
*By invitation / selection*

A fast-paced, plenary lightning round over lunch that puts **emerging data talent** in front of
the full Data Day audience. The Showcase features interns from **MORPC's Summer Internship
Program**, who are placed with local governments and institutions across the region and bring
fresh perspective to real public-sector data work.

- **Format:** short lightning talks (~5–7 minutes each), presented to the full room during lunch.
- **Who's eligible:** participants in MORPC's Summer Internship Program.
- **How it works:** interns submit their projects; a selection committee chooses a few for the
  lightning talks. **Projects not selected for a talk are invited to present as posters** (see
  below) — one submission, two ways to shine.

### Student poster Call for Proposals
*Open call for proposals — Central Ohio students*

We invite **college, university, and graduate students** from across the region to submit posters
sharing their data-driven research, analysis, or applied projects. The student poster CFP is a
chance to share your work with the region's data community, get feedback from practitioners, and

- **Who can propose:** currently enrolled college, university, and graduate students at Central
  Ohio institutions.
- **Format:** printed poster, displayed all day; you'll staff it during attended poster times.
- **What to tell us:** title, abstract, your program/institution, and advisor (if any).

## What we'll ask you to submit (by format)

A quick preview of the questions each submission form will cover. Items marked *(internal)* help
MORPC staff understand and balance the program — they aren't printed in the agenda.

### Lecture
- Applicant name and contact (primary contact for the proposal)
- Speaker(s) and organizational affiliations
- Session title (≤10 words)
- Up to three session objectives — what the audience will gain or learn
- Session description (≤100 words) — the "why attend this session?" blurb for the brochure
- Session abstract (longer) — content, topics, format, intended audience, expected outcomes
- Intended audience (e.g., decision-makers, data professionals, subject-matter experts, other)
- Technical skill level (0–3)
- Regional Data Agenda **focus area** *(internal)*
- Regional Data Agenda **action pillar** *(internal)*
- Reflection: *"How does your session advance [focus area] as a regional data priority?"* *(internal)*
- How you'll engage the audience / encourage participation
- AV and technology needs

### Workshop
*Everything in the lecture list, plus the hands-on specifics:*
- Learning goals — what participants will build or be able to do afterward
- Tools and software used
- Prerequisites — what attendees should already know
- Required setup beforehand (installs, accounts) and whether a laptop is needed
- Ideal group size / any participant cap
- Special room, power, or AV needs

### Panel *(suggestion)*
- Your name and contact (the person suggesting the panel)
- Proposed panel topic / working title
- Why this topic matters to the region now
- Key questions or angles the panel would explore
- Suggested moderator and/or panelists with affiliations (optional)
- *(Focus area, action pillar, and skill level are assigned by staff.)*

### Intern Showcase — lightning talk or poster
*One submission; the committee selects lightning talks, and remaining projects become posters.*
- Intern name and contact
- Internship host organization / placement
- Project title
- Project summary — the question, the data, what you did, and the result
- Why it matters — impact for the host organization or community
- Any visuals or slides you'd use (optional)

### Student poster
- Student name and contact
- Institution and program; advisor (if any)
- Poster title
- Abstract — research/project summary: question, data and methods, findings or status
- *(Posters carry no skill/focus tags; see poster specs for size and format.)*
