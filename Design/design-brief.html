Visual and Color Guide

The classic Data Day palette, retained for the 5th annual event. This guide covers the core colors, the extended scales, which combinations are approved, and how they apply to web and print materials.

  • Version 1.0
  • Updated 21 July 2026
  • Owner MORPC / Regional Data Roundtable

01 · The decision

What changed, and what didn't.

Design review chose continuity. Data Day keeps its established palette and logo for the 5th annual event, so the anniversary framing carries the message rather than a rebrand competing with it. The four brand colors below are taken directly from the logo artwork, so marks drop into any layout without a color correction step.

Two colors were added, because four brand colors cannot build a document on their own: a dark muted black for body text and reversed surfaces, and a very light grey to give white cards something to sit on.

Nothing else was introduced. Every tint and shade in this guide is derived from the four core hexes.

02 · Core palette

Six colors do all the work.

Dark Blue carries structure. Blue carries anything interactive. Teal and Green are accents, held back so a single call to action stays obvious on any page. Ink and Fog are the surfaces.

Dark Blue #073451 Primary. Headlines, nav, footers, dark bands.
Blue #0977BC Secondary. Links, buttons, section accents.
Teal #00A79D Accent. Primary CTA fill, highlights, chart series.
Green #7CB46A Accent. Secondary CTA, tags, chart series.
Ink #171C21 Dark muted black. Body text, reversed surfaces.
Fog #F2F4F6 Very light grey. Page background, card fill.

03 · Extended scales

100 to 700.

Each brand color extends to a seven-step scale, with 500 as the core hex. Tints (100 to 400) are fills, washes, and text on dark surfaces. Shades (600 to 700) are text, links, and pressed states on light surfaces. This is where the accessibility work happens: the bright accents cannot carry text on white, and the 600 and 700 steps exist to cover exactly that.

04 · Accessibility

The two traps

  • Teal 500 and Green 500 cannot be text on light backgrounds. They land at 2.99:1 and 2.44:1 on white. They are fill colors there. For teal text use Teal 600, for green text use Green 700.
  • Blue 500 is not a link color. On the Fog page background it is 4.35:1 and misses AA. Links on light use Blue 600 (7.56:1 on white). On dark, Blue 500 drops to 3.57:1, so links there use Blue 300 (8.57:1 on Ink).

On the Dark Blue band, step accents up one stop. Teal 500 on Dark Blue is only 4.33:1, which is large-text and UI only. Teal 300 on the same band is 7.67:1 and is safe for any text size.

Do and don't

Teal 600 as text on white
Do 2.99:1 becomes 5.08:1 by stepping to the 600 shade. Passes AA.
Teal 500 as text on white
Don't 2.99:1. Fails at every text size. Teal 500 is a fill on light surfaces.
Ink text on a Teal button
Do 5.73:1. The accents are bright, so they take dark text.
White text on a Teal button
Don't 2.99:1. If a teal button needs white text, use Teal 600 (5.08:1).
Teal 300 on the Dark Blue band
Do 7.67:1. Accents step up one stop on the navy band.
Teal 500 on the Dark Blue band
Don't 4.33:1. Large text and UI only, never body copy.

Print rules
Ink and paper

Dark Blue title, Blue 600 headings, Ink body on white. Teal for the title rule and accent bars only.

Reversed panels

Use the White logo lockup on Dark Blue or Ink. Never place the Primary lockup on a dark panel.

Grayscale fallback

Teal and Green convert to near-identical greys. If a document may be photocopied, separate categories by label or pattern, not color alone.

Minimum sizes

Horizontal lockup no smaller than 1.25 in wide in print, 120 px on screen.

Categorical: attendance by sector

Multi-series: pair color with a second channel

Attendees Organizations Sessions

Sequential: one measure, low to high

Use the Blue scale for choropleths and heatmaps. For a second measure use the Teal scale. Do not mix two hues into one sequential ramp.

Blue and Teal are the closest pair under deuteranopia. For two-series charts use Blue and Green. When series must be told apart at a glance, add a second channel: dash pattern, marker shape, or direct labels.

09 · Logo

Lockups and clear space.

Four files live in Design/logo/: Primary and White, each in horizontal and vertical. Pick by background, never by recoloring.

Primary, horizontalWhite and Fog backgrounds. The default for print.
White, horizontalDark Blue and Ink backgrounds. The default for web.
Primary, verticalSquare and narrow spaces: social avatars, signage, badges.
Clear spaceKeep clear space on all sides equal to the height of the mark. Nothing enters the dashed zone.

Logo don'ts

  • Do not recolor the mark. The four colors in the artwork are the brand.
  • Do not place the Primary lockup on Dark Blue, Ink, or any photograph. Use the White lockup.
  • Do not stretch, rotate, add effects, or rebuild the lockup from parts.
  • Do not set the horizontal lockup below 1.25 in wide in print, or 120 px on screen.
  • Co-brand with MORPC by placing the MORPC logo at the opposite edge with equal optical weight, never inside the Data Day clear space.

10 · Reference

Copy and paste.

Full token set, including all 100 to 700 scales and semantic aliases, lives in Design/palette.css. Documentation and the full contrast tables are in Design/palette.md. These are the values you need most often.

/* Core */
--dd-navy-500:  #073451;  /* Dark Blue: headlines, nav, bands */
--dd-blue-500:  #0977BC;  /* Blue: buttons, fills */
--dd-teal-500:  #00A79D;  /* Teal: primary CTA fill */
--dd-green-500: #7CB46A;  /* Green: secondary accent */
--dd-ink:       #171C21;  /* body text, dark surfaces */
--dd-fog:       #F2F4F6;  /* page background */

/* Text-safe steps: use these, not the 500s */
--dd-blue-600:  #07588B;  /* links on light  (7.56:1 on white) */
--dd-teal-600:  #007C74;  /* teal text on light */
--dd-green-700: #425F38;  /* green text on light */
--dd-blue-300:  #89BEDF;  /* links on dark   (8.57:1 on Ink) */
--dd-teal-300:  #85D5D0;  /* accents on the navy band */

/* Supporting neutrals */
--dd-slate:     #5F6B75;  /* captions, metadata */
--dd-mist:      #DDE2E7;  /* hairlines, table borders */
--dd-ink-soft:  #2A3238;  /* elevated dark surface */

The short version

  • Dark Blue for structure, Blue for anything clickable, Teal for the one action that matters.
  • Body text is Ink on light, Fog on dark. Captions are Slate on light, Dark Blue 300 on dark.
  • Accents at 500 are fills. When an accent must be text, step to 600 or 700 on light, 300 on dark.
  • White logo on dark, Primary logo on light. Never recolor either.
